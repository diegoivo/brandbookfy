# Brand Archetypes Reference

The 12 Jungian archetypes adapted for brand identity. Each archetype implies visual direction — colors, typography weight, spatial composition, and motion style.

## The 12 Archetypes

### 1. The Innocent
**Core desire**: Safety, happiness, simplicity
**Traits**: Optimistic, honest, wholesome, trustworthy
**Visual cues**: Light palettes, rounded forms, generous whitespace, soft shadows, warm tones
**Typography**: Friendly sans-serif (rounded terminals), moderate weight
**Color direction**: Pastels, warm whites, sky blue, soft green
**Brands**: Coca-Cola, Dove, Nintendo

### 2. The Sage
**Core desire**: Truth, understanding, wisdom
**Traits**: Analytical, thoughtful, authoritative, informed
**Visual cues**: Clean grids, data-rich layouts, precise spacing, restrained palette
**Typography**: Humanist sans + serif for authority; mono for data
**Color direction**: Deep navy, slate, muted teal, parchment
**Brands**: Google, BBC, MIT

### 3. The Explorer
**Core desire**: Freedom, discovery, adventure
**Traits**: Bold, independent, ambitious, pioneering
**Visual cues**: Expansive layouts, dramatic photography, full-bleed sections, diagonal elements
**Typography**: Strong geometric sans, condensed headings, wide tracking
**Color direction**: Earth tones, deep greens, amber, desert ochre
**Brands**: Jeep, Patagonia, The North Face

### 4. The Outlaw (Rebel)
**Core desire**: Revolution, disruption, liberation
**Traits**: Disruptive, edgy, countercultural, provocative
**Visual cues**: High contrast, broken grids, asymmetry, raw textures, dark themes
**Typography**: Bold/black weight sans, condensed, tight tracking, all-caps display
**Color direction**: Black, red, electric accents, neon on dark
**Brands**: Harley-Davidson, Diesel, Virgin

### 5. The Magician
**Core desire**: Transformation, making dreams real
**Traits**: Visionary, charismatic, imaginative, transformative
**Visual cues**: Depth layers, subtle gradients (not on logos), mysterious shadows, reveal animations
**Typography**: Elegant serif display + modern sans body, contrasting weights
**Color direction**: Deep violet, midnight blue, gold accents, rich jewel tones
**Brands**: Apple, Disney, Tesla

### 6. The Hero
**Core desire**: Mastery, proving worth through courage
**Traits**: Determined, strong, confident, inspiring
**Visual cues**: Bold geometry, strong vertical lines, decisive spacing, prominent CTAs
**Typography**: Heavy-weight geometric sans, strong hierarchy, uppercase headings
**Color direction**: Deep blue, crimson, black, silver metallic
**Brands**: Nike, FedEx, BMW

### 7. The Lover
**Core desire**: Intimacy, passion, sensory experience
**Traits**: Passionate, elegant, sophisticated, sensual
**Visual cues**: Luxurious textures, warm lighting effects, flowing curves, rich photography
**Typography**: High-contrast serif display, elegant italic, refined sans body
**Color direction**: Deep burgundy, rose, gold, warm neutrals, rich cream
**Brands**: Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Godiva

### 8. The Jester
**Core desire**: Joy, living in the moment, fun
**Traits**: Playful, humorous, irreverent, entertaining
**Visual cues**: Bright colors, irregular shapes, hand-drawn elements, bouncy animations, collage
**Typography**: Rounded/quirky display, mixed weights, playful tracking, custom letterforms
**Color direction**: Saturated primaries, unexpected combinations, electric palette
**Brands**: Old Spice, M&M's, Dollar Shave Club

### 9. The Everyperson
**Core desire**: Belonging, connection, fitting in
**Traits**: Relatable, down-to-earth, friendly, inclusive
**Visual cues**: Comfortable layouts, familiar patterns, accessible imagery, warm tones
**Typography**: Friendly humanist sans, moderate weight, generous line-height
**Color direction**: Warm greys, soft blue, muted green, natural tones
**Brands**: IKEA, Target, Gap

### 10. The Caregiver
**Core desire**: Protecting and caring for others
**Traits**: Compassionate, nurturing, generous, selfless
**Visual cues**: Soft edges, rounded corners, ample padding, calming motion, warm imagery
**Typography**: Rounded sans, medium weight, open letterforms, generous spacing
**Color direction**: Soft teal, warm peach, gentle lavender, sage green
**Brands**: Johnson & Johnson, UNICEF, Campbell's

### 11. The Ruler
**Core desire**: Control, order, stability, success
**Traits**: Authoritative, commanding, refined, responsible
**Visual cues**: Structured grids, symmetry, luxurious spacing, strong borders, dark themes
**Typography**: Classic serif display, structured sans headings, formal hierarchy
**Color direction**: Navy, gold, deep charcoal, burgundy, black with metallic accents
**Brands**: Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, Microsoft

### 12. The Creator
**Core desire**: Innovation, self-expression, vision
**Traits**: Creative, inventive, artistic, non-conformist
**Visual cues**: Unique layouts, creative compositions, bold experimentation, artistic touches
**Typography**: Distinctive display (custom or unusual), creative pairings, variable weights
**Color direction**: Depends on medium — often bold + unexpected combinations
**Brands**: Adobe, LEGO, YouTube

## Choosing an Archetype

Most brands are a **primary + secondary** blend. The primary drives 80% of visual decisions; the secondary adds nuance.

Common pairings:
- **Sage + Magician** = Visionary intelligence (tech/SaaS)
- **Hero + Explorer** = Bold ambition (fitness, adventure)
- **Lover + Ruler** = Luxurious authority (premium brands)
- **Creator + Outlaw** = Disruptive innovation (startups)
- **Caregiver + Innocent** = Trustworthy warmth (healthcare, family)
- **Jester + Everyperson** = Approachable fun (consumer brands)

## From Archetype to Visual Direction

Once the archetype is chosen, use it to inform:

1. **Color temperature**: Warm (Lover, Caregiver, Innocent) vs Cool (Sage, Ruler, Explorer)
2. **Typography weight**: Heavy (Hero, Outlaw, Ruler) vs Light (Lover, Innocent, Sage)
3. **Spatial density**: Dense (Creator, Jester) vs Spacious (Ruler, Sage, Innocent)
4. **Corner radius**: Sharp (Outlaw, Hero, Ruler) vs Rounded (Innocent, Caregiver, Jester)
5. **Motion style**: Dramatic (Magician, Outlaw) vs Subtle (Sage, Ruler) vs Bouncy (Jester)
6. **Theme default**: Dark-first (Outlaw, Magician, Explorer) vs Light-first (Innocent, Caregiver, Sage)
