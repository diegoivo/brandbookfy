Brand Identity System

PixelFrge

Narrative-driven pixel art RPGs forged by hand. A visual identity for the studio that turns 8-bit nostalgia into modern storytelling.

01 — Archetype

Brand Personality

PixelForge embodies the Creator archetype with Jester influence -- inventive and artistic at the core, with a playful irreverence that keeps things from getting too serious.

The Creator + Jester

Inventive Artistic Playful Irreverent

Creator brands build things that didn't exist before. They value self-expression, experimentation, and the craft of making. The Jester influence adds humor and wit -- PixelForge doesn't take itself too seriously even when the work is serious. The brand speaks to those who see games as an art form worth defending.

Tone
Confident but never arrogant. Casual like talking to a friend who happens to be ridiculously talented at making games.
Language
Technical when it matters, irreverent by default. Avoids corporate jargon. Speaks in the language of its community.
Emotion
Nostalgic warmth mixed with rebellious energy. The feeling of discovering a secret level at 2 a.m.

02 — Logo & Wordmark

Wordmark Variants

The PixelForge wordmark uses Unbounded at 800 weight. The "o" in "Forge" is replaced with a solid pixel block in the primary color -- a nod to the studio's pixel art craft. This block is the core identity element.

Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the cap-height of the "P" in "Pixel" on all sides of the wordmark. This ensures the mark has breathing room and never feels crowded by surrounding elements.

06 — Don't

Negative Rules

These are explicit violations of the PixelForge brand system. Each rule protects the brand's integrity and consistency.

Don't
PixelForge

Never stretch or distort the wordmark. Always use it at its original proportions.

Don't
PixelForge

Never apply gradients to the wordmark. The logo must always use flat, solid colors.

Don't
PixelForge

Never use unapproved fonts for the wordmark. It must always use Unbounded 800.

Don't
This text is far too small to read comfortably on any screen.

Never set body text below 14px (0.875rem). Readability is non-negotiable, even in pixel art.

Don't
PixelForge

Never use unapproved color combinations on the wordmark. Only the defined variants are allowed.

Don't

Never remove focus indicators from interactive elements. Keyboard users depend on visible focus rings.
