Brand Guidelines

Lex§

Contract review and compliance automation for small law firms across Latin America. Authoritative precision meets accessible technology.

01 — Archetype

Brand Personality

The Sage archetype grounds Lex§ in wisdom and analytical clarity. A secondary Ruler influence adds the structured authority that legal professionals expect from their tools.

The Sage · Ruler

Analytical Authoritative Structured Trustworthy

Lex§ speaks with earned authority, never condescension. The brand communicates complex legal processes with clarity and precision, making contract review feel systematic rather than overwhelming. Every interaction reinforces competence and reliability.

Voice Guidelines

Precise, Not Verbose
Use exact legal terminology when it adds clarity. Cut filler words. Every sentence earns its place.
Confident, Not Rigid
State findings and recommendations directly. Avoid hedging language. But acknowledge nuance when the law requires it.
Guiding, Not Lecturing
Walk users through compliance requirements step by step. Explain the why behind each flag, not just the what.
Human, Not Stuffy
These are tech-savvy attorneys running lean firms. Speak to peers, not students. Drop the formality when the context allows it.

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the cap-height of the "L" around all sides of the wordmark. This ensures legibility at all sizes and prevents visual crowding from adjacent elements.

06 — Don't

Negative Rules

These are explicit violations of the Lex§ brand system. Enforcing these constraints preserves the authoritative, trustworthy identity that legal professionals expect.

Don't
Lex§

Never stretch, rotate, or distort the wordmark. The section sign must remain at its designed proportions.

Don't
Lex§

Never apply gradients to the logo. The wordmark must always use flat, solid color to maintain legibility across all contexts.

Don't
Lex§

Never replace the designated typeface with system fonts. The judicial authority of DM Serif Display and Albert Sans is essential to the brand.

Don't
This clause text is unreadable at this size. Accessibility is non-negotiable in legal tools.

Never set body text below 14px (0.875rem). Attorneys depend on this tool for contract review -- readability is a legal requirement.

Don't
Approve Contract

Never use color combinations that fail WCAG AA contrast. Dark text on dark backgrounds makes critical actions invisible.

Don't
Decorative Display Text

Never use the display serif for decorative purposes at extreme sizes. Reserve DM Serif Display for headings and the wordmark only.
