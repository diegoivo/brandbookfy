Brand Guidelines

stillpoint

Science-backed meditation and breathwork for professionals who want evidence, not dogma. Calm without the incense.

01 — Archetype

Brand Personality

stillpoint blends the Innocent's yearning for simplicity and inner peace with the Sage's commitment to evidence and understanding. The result is a brand that feels grounded and restorative without mysticism.

The Innocent

with Sage influence

Grounded Evidence-based Calm Trustworthy

The Innocent archetype gives stillpoint its warmth, simplicity, and sense of safe haven. The Sage influence ensures every claim is backed by research and every design choice communicates credibility. Together, they create a brand that skeptics trust and stressed professionals return to.

Voice Guidelines

Tone
Quiet confidence. Speak with authority but never lecture. Prefer short, clear sentences.
Language
Plain and precise. Say "reduces cortisol" instead of "healing energy." Cite studies, not traditions.
Perspective
Peer, not guru. Address users as capable adults who happen to need a better way to manage stress.
Avoid
Spiritual jargon, Sanskrit terms, "journey" metaphors, exclamation marks, all-caps enthusiasm.

02 — Logo & Wordmark

Wordmark System

The all-lowercase "stillpoint" is itself the identity. No capitals, no punctuation, no icon. The absence of visual noise mirrors the product's purpose: a place where activity stops and presence begins.

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the cap-height of the wordmark on all sides. This breathing room is essential to the brand's sense of calm.

06 — Don't

Negative Rules

These violations undermine the stillpoint brand. Each one erodes the trust and calm that define the product experience.

Don't
stillpoint

Never stretch or distort the wordmark. The letterforms are designed with specific proportions that reflect the brand's composure.

Don't
stillpoint

Never apply gradients to the wordmark. The logo must always be a flat, solid color for clarity and calm.

Don't
stillpoint

Never render the wordmark in an unapproved font. The serif character of Cormorant is integral to the brand.

Don't
STILLPOINT

Never capitalize the name. The all-lowercase form is the brand. Uppercase contradicts the identity's quiet nature.

Don't
This text is far too small for comfortable reading on any screen.

Never set body text below 14px (0.875rem). The brand values accessibility over visual density.

Don't
zen

Never use the brand fonts decoratively at extreme sizes or pair them with spiritual/esoteric language. The system exists to communicate, not to decorate.
